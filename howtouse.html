How to Use

Step 1: Setting Up

1

Before you can start using the device, you must create an account. If you don't already have one, please navigate to the Sign Up page of this website to create one.

2

You'll be asked for your email, name, phone number, and password. All fields must be completed to sign up.

3

Once you sign in, to get and analyze data, you'll need our two apps: Foresight Image Collection and Foresight Blink Detection. Download the files here. To run the Foresight Image Collection App, you'll also need a JRE. Many computers come with it preinstalled, but if you aren't sure if you have it, download it here.

4

Once the app files are downloaded, go into File Explorer on your computer and unzip the folder. (You can unzip it by right clicking and selecting "Extract files".)

5

Enter the unzipped folder and find the ForesightImageCapture and ForesightBlinkDetection files. You'll be using the files here for analysis later on. If you're running on an older computer, you may also need to download a to be able to execute the ForsightImageCapture app.

Step 2: Wearing the Device

1

Fasten the box to your chest as shown by wrapping the strap above one shoulder and below the other. The side with the black tube should be facing away from your chest.

2

Buckle the strap and tighten to your liking.

3

Place the glasses onto your head as you normally would. For comfort, you may want to place the black cord around/behind your neck rather than in front of it.

4

Plug the non-USB end of the grey wire into the Arduino through the hole on the side of the box on your chest.

5

Plug the USB end of the grey wire into your computer.

Step 3: Running the Software

1

Like you did when setting up, go back into the folder containing the program files and enter the "EXECUTE ME" folder.

2

Double click on the file titled "ForesightBlinkDetection" to open it.

3

Read the instructions, enter your email and password into the interface, and click "Sign In" to log in. (If you don't have an account, sign up here.)

4

Read the new instructions that pop up, and click "Start Analysis" when you're ready. A new screen will pop up telling you to start taking images using Foresight Image Capture.

5

Once you have signed in successfully, navigate back to the folder where you found the "ForesightBlinkDetection" file and double click on the file titled "ForesightImageCapture" to open it.

6

In the top left corner you should see the name of the USB port that your Arduino is connected to. If you don't see this, close the Image Capture app, reconnect the USB to your computer, and reopen the app. Once you're in the app, new port connections will not be updated, so don't change where the device is plugged in.

7

When you're ready, select "Begin session" to start gathering images. Images will be shown to you as they are captured. Don't be alarmed by the green image at the beginning--it's used for calibration! After the green image, you should see images of your face being loaded in. If the lighting in your location is too dark or too bright, the camera will take a few frames to adjust to it.

8

The images will be automatically retrieved by the Foresight Blink Detection software and displayed again in a new window as they are analyzed. You'll see an outline of your eyes, the number of blinks in the session, and your eye aspect ratio (EAR)! You'll get an SMS notification if you're detected to be tired. (You're likely tired if you blink at least 4 times for at least 2 minutes in succession.)

9

When you're done, you may click "End session" on Foresight Image Capture to stop gathering images. (You can click "Start session" again to save data in a new session.)

10

To stop blink analysis, press "Stop Analysis" while on Foresight Blink Detection. On the new page that comes up, you can click "Back to Analysis" again to start over. (Don't click "Stop Analysis" before you click "End Session" because this prevent subsequent images from being analyzed.)

Step 4: Viewing Your Data

1

To view your data, sign in first and then navigate to the Statistics page of this site.

2

From the dropdown shown, select what session you'd like to view your data for.

3

Click "Enter", and you'll be shown a graph of how your blinks per minute varied over time, as well as your average blinks per minute and the number of minutes that you were detected to be tired for.

4

To view data for other sessions, go back to the dropdown, select a new session, and click "Enter" again.
